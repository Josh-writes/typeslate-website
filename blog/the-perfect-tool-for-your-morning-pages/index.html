The Perfect Tool for Your Morning Pages

I have a confession: I DNF’d The Artist’s Way. Not because it isn’t a good book — it is. But I wasn’t looking for a workbook when I picked it up, and that’s exactly what it is. I wanted broad, conceptual ideas about creativity and writing, not something that demanded weekly assignments and check-ins.

I’m sure at some point in my life I’ll be ready to actually do the work of the book. But at the time? No way. Especially the Artist Date. I’m a preschool teacher and a father of two. The idea of taking myself out on a solo creative date was laughably unrealistic. The only time I get for myself is between 5:00 and 7:00 AM, before the rest of the house wakes up.

But speaking of 5 to 7 AM...

One thing I did take from The Artist’s Way, and still swear by, is Morning Pages.

Handwriting wasn’t an option for me. I don’t handwrite anything unless it’s a sticky note or a quick scribble. There’s no way I was going to fill up a handwritten journal every morning.

So for a while, I used Word. I’d open my “Brain Purge” document and try to hit 750 words — roughly the typed equivalent of three pages. I’d scroll, highlight, subtract word counts, guess where I started. It worked… kind of. But it was clunky. And every distraction in Word reminded me it wasn’t made for journaling.

So I built something better.

That’s how TypeSlate was born.

Now, I open TypeSlate, set the folder to Morning Journal, and turn on Word Count mode. I type in 750, hit start, and the screen goes dark. No distractions. Just me and the page.

Most of the 100,000+ words I’ve written in TypeSlate are journal entries. It’s the perfect tool for clearing your head. No spellcheck. No grammar squiggles. No judgment.

Just writing.

If you're like me — someone who journals to clear the noise, track ideas, or just show up to the page — you need to try TypeSlate.

One other feature I love: autocopy. When I finish journaling, I paste the entry into ChatGPT. That’s where it becomes my assistant, editor, therapist, or hype man. I don’t journal about anything too personal, so I don’t mind sharing it. But if you’re someone who goes deep, maybe be thoughtful about where you paste it.

Either way, morning pages (or just plain journaling) changed my writing life.

And TypeSlate made it way easier to stick with.
